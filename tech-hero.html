Miriam Rivera

ULU Ventures CEO

Biography

Miriam Riveria is the Co-founder and CEO of ULU Ventures which holds 60 million of startups. She started her journey at Standford University where she obtained her degree. She has achieved the Standford Medal of Excellence and has been teaching at Standford for her incredible work. About half a percent of all Stanford graduates are given this award. Now she is currently Stanford's lead Council and works in teaching law students about what it takes to be a lawyer. Her current goal is to invest in for-profit companies that are helping the needs of low-income Americans. Before starting her own company she was the Vice President of the general council at Google. Almost every single major deal from her tenure was signed by her. She helped Google's portfolio go from 85 million dollars to 10 Billion.

Impact on Tech and Society

Mirriam worked as a lawyer and consultant most of her life. However, what she did for tech was advance the largest tech company from nothing to the best. Her work as a lawyer helped make Google an innovator and the golden standard of what a tech company is supposed to be. As a CEO she helped invest in startups for low-income individuals to make their dreams come true. This made it easier for the lower class to move up in the world as it gave them loans for businesses that otherwise would get denied for low credit.

Challenging the Status Quo

Mirriam worked as the lead general counsel for the largest tech company in the world. Not only was she able to achieve one of the greatest lawyer positions on the planet she was able to do so while being a Latina woman. Despite sexism, racism, and other forms of discrimination. On top of that she founded her own company that was incredibly successful and remains one of the few Laina women CEOs. Mirriam Rivera remains one of the most successful women on the planet. p>

Jasmine Crowe-Houston

Founder and CEO of Goodr.

Biography

Jasmine Crowe-Houston is the founder and CEO of Goodr, a sustainable food surplus management corporation committed to leveraging technology to reduce food waste and feed the hungry. Goodr aims to make it easy for companies to donate food by enabling them to track, manage, and redistribute surplus food efficiently.

Impact on Tech and Society

Jasmine launched Goodr. with one thing on her mind which was to offer hunger relief and waste relief for residents of her local city Atlanta who have had significant homelessness in the past decade. The way that she does this is by finding where the extra food is and shipping it to places that have food scarcity all across the world. As of 2022 Jasmine's company has been able to serve 30 Million meals to those in need as well as move over 1 million pounds of materials out of landfills and into recycle plants.

Challenging the Status Quo

Jasmine consistently challenges the status quo as being a woman of color in the business world consistently faces discrimination and sexism that makes owning a company much more difficult. In Atlanta her hometown also is challenged with the repercussions of redlining and suburbanization of the area where she was able to overcome these challenges
